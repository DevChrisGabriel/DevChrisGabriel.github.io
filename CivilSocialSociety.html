Global Civil Society

Global civil society- Civil society emerged in the 1980s as a realm separate from the state and economy, crucial for democratization. It includes independent social groups like religious and cultural organizations. While traditionally tied to the nation-state, its role in international politics differs, connecting autonomous groups globally and challenging states. Global civil society influences world governance by fostering cooperation, supporting humanitarian goals, and shaping international law. This chapter focuses on global organizations with cosmopolitan aims, showcasing their impact on global citizenship.

Origin and Importance:

Emerged in the 1980s, crucial for democratization, especially in resisting state control in Eastern Europe.

Role in Global Governance:

Influences world governance by fostering international cooperation, supporting humanitarian goals, and shaping emerging international law.

National vs. Global Perspective:

Traditionally linked to the nation-state; however, in international politics, it transcends borders and challenges states when groups unite globally.

Scope:

Encompasses religious, cultural, and charitable organizations, as well as interest groups and informal networks.

Political implications:

Can have political implications, especially in totalitarian states where any independent social activity challenges the regime.
